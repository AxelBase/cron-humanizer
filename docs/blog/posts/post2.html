Breaking Down Cron Fields

Breaking Down Cron Fields

Published: 12 November 2025 • AxelBase

Every cron expression is built from five time fields. Understanding their constraints and behaviors is critical for reliable scheduling.

1. Minute (0–59)

First field. Supports values from 0 to 59. Common patterns:

  • 0 — Start of hour
  • 30 — Half past
  • */15 — Every 15 minutes
  • 1,16,31,46 — Specific minutes

Pitfall

60 is invalid — minute max is 59.

2. Hour (0–23)

Uses 24-hour format. Midnight is 0, noon is 12.

  • 9 — 9:00 AM
  • 21 — 9:00 PM
  • 9-17 — Business hours

3. Day of Month (1–31)

Specifies calendar day. Use ? when using day of week.

4. Month (1–12)

Supports numeric or names (JAN–DEC in some systems).

5. Day of Week (0–7)

Both 0 and 7 represent Sunday. Use ? when using day of month.

Precision starts with understanding the fields.
